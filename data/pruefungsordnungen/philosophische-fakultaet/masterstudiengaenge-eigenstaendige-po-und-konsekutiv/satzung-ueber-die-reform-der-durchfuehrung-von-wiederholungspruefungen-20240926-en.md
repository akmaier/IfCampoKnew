## **Regulations on the reform of resit examinations at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) dated August 26, 2024** 

Based on Section 9 (1) in conjunction with Section 80 (1)(1) and Section 84 (2)(1) Bavarian Higher Education Innovation Act dated August 5, 2022 ( **BayHIG** ), FAU enacts the following regulations: 

## **Preamble** 

1These regulations aim to reform the procedure for resit examinations in the degree programs at FAU pursuant to Section 77 **BayHIG** .[2] The purpose of the reform is to ease examination conditions and emphasize students’ responsibility for organizing their studies independently.[3] To achieve these goals, mandatory registration for resit examinations which was previously widespread at FAU is to be abolished, and students will in the future register for resit examinations in a semester of their choice and, unlike before, will also be able to withdraw from resit examinations within the usual deadlines.[4] Furthermore, with the exception of the examinations of the Grundlagenund Orientierungsprüfung (GOP), other intermediate examinations and final theses, an additional resit attempt is to be granted.[5] To ensure teaching resources and effective planning, exceptions should be possible, especially for internships, field seminars, excursions, and similar courses, where in the case of a resit, the class must also be repeated.[6] The changes apply to all examinations (first, second, or third attempt) conducted from the winter semester 2024/2025 onwards.[7] Procedures for clarifying facts and providing evidence in cases of suspected deception are also expanded.[ 8] To implement this as efficiently as possible, the degree program and examination regulations to be amended that are not already being changed for the winter semester 2024/2025 will be adjusted simultaneously within the framework of a collective amendment statute.
