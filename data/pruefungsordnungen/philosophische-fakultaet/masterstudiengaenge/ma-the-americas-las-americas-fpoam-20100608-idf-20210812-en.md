## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree program and examination regulations for the research-oriented consecutive Master’s degree program in The Americas/Las Américas leading to the degree Master of Arts (MA) supplement the valid version of the General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor’s and Master’s Degree Programs at the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology at FAU – **ABMStPO/Phil** –. 

1 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master’s Degree, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] A subject-specific degree within the meaning of Section 35 (1)(1) **ABMStPO/Phil** shall be a degree in a one-subject, two-subject or three- subject Bachelor’s degree program in American Studies or English/American Studies and/or Hispanic Studies or Latin American Studies.[2] One-subject, two-subject or three-subject Bachelor’s degrees shall be recognised as subject-related degrees within the meaning of Section 35 (1) (1) in conjunction with paragraph 2 **ABMStPO/Phil** provided knowledge relating to the specific region (North America and/or Latin America) accounting for at least 60 ECTS credits was obtained. Diplom and Magister degrees or comparable international degrees in regional-specific subjects and examinations passed as part of the First State Examination in teaching in English and/or Spanish with a corresponding number of ECTS credits in the areas relating to the specific regions shall also be recognised as subject-related degrees. 

(2) Evidence of proficiency in English at a level equivalent to at least C1 of the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) and B2 in Spanish or vice versa shall be submitted together with the application documents pursuant to Section (2) (2) (2) **Appendix 1 ABMStPO/Phil** . 

(3)[1] Applicants who cannot be admitted directly to the program pursuant to Section 5 (2) **Appendix 1 ABMStPO/Phil** and who have an average grade of achievements to date of between 2.51 and 4.00 may be admitted to the Master's degree program in The Americas/Las Americas on the basis of a selection interview. 2The selection interview shall demonstrate that the applicant possesses the required technical and methodological expertise and can be expected to carry out independent academic work in a more research-oriented degree program.[3] In the selection interview, applicants shall be evaluated according to the following criteria, which are weighted as specified: 

1. Quality of academic knowledge specific to the region (25%), 

2. A positive prognosis demonstrated by the applicant’s academic progress (discussion on the basis of documents from the applicant’s previous degree, in particular the transcript of records) (15%), 

3. Analytical skills (20%), 

4. Interpretation skills (20%), 

5. Ability to explore various aspects of an issue (20%). 

## **Section 3 Scope and Structure of the Master’s Degree Program, Examinations, Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1) The scope and structure of the Master’s degree program in The Americas/Las Américas as well as the scope and type of examinations are set forth in the **Appendix** . 

(2)[1] Contrary to Section 5 (5) **ABMStPO/Phil** , the teaching and examination languages in the Master’s degree program in The Americas/Las Américas are English and Spanish.[2] Individual compulsory elective modules are offered in German, but options in English and Spanish shall always be offered as an alternative.[3] Further details are stipulated in the module handbook.[4] In any case of uncertainty, the examination language shall be the same as the teaching language.

## **Section 4 Legal Validity and Transitory Provisions** 

(1) These degree program and examination regulations shall come into effect on the day after their publication. 

(2)[1] The amendment statute shall come into effect on the day after its publication.[2] It shall apply to all students starting a degree program from winter semester 2018/2019 onwards. 

(3)[1] The seventh amendment statute shall come into effect on October 1, 2021.[2] It shall apply to all students starting a degree program from winter semester 2021/2022 onwards.[3] Examinations according to the previously valid degree program and examination regulations shall be offered for the last time in summer semester 2026. 4From the date stated in sentence 3, those students who are affected by the examination regulations becoming invalid shall take their examinations in accordance with the currently valid version of the degree program and examination regulations.
