## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree programme and examination regulations for the research-orientated consecutive Master's degree programme in The Americas/Las Américas leading to the degree Master of Arts (M.A.) supplement the valid version of the General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's Degree Programmes at the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology at FAU – **ABMStPO/Phil** . 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master's Degree, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] A subject-specific degree within the meaning of Section 35 (1)(1) **ABMStPO/Phil** shall be a degree in a one-subject, two-subject or three- subject Bachelor’s degree 

1 

programme in American Studies or English/American Studies and/or Hispanic Studies or Latin American Studies.[2] One-subject, two-subject or three-subject Bachelor's degrees shall be recognised as subject-related degrees within the meaning of Section 35 (1) (1) in conjunction with paragraph 2 **ABMStPO/Phil** provided knowledge relating to the specific region (North America and/or Latin America) accounting for at least 60 ECTS credits was obtained. Diplom and Magister degrees or comparable international degrees in regional-specific subjects and examinations passed as part of the First State Examination in teaching in English and/or Spanish with a corresponding number of ECTS credits in the areas relating to the specific regions shall also be recognised as subject-related degrees. 

(2) Evidence of proficiency in English at a level equivalent to at least C1 of the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) and B2 in Spanish or vice versa shall be submitted together with application documents pursuant to Section (2) (2) (2) Appendix 1 **ABMStPO/Phil** . 

(3)[1] Applicants with an overall grade in their subject-specific or subject-related degree or, in the case of Section 35 (4) **ABMStPO/Phil** , an average of their achievements hitherto of between 2.51 and 4.00 may be admitted to the Master's degree programme in The Americas/Las Américas on the basis of a selection interview.[2] The selection interview is intended to demonstrate that the applicant possesses the required technical and methodological expertise and can be expected to carry out independent academic work in a more research-orientated degree programme.[3] In the selection interview, applicants shall be evaluated according to the following criteria, which are weighted as specified: 

1. Quality of academic knowledge specific to the region (25 %), 

2. A positive prognosis based on improving progress during the applicant's course of studies (discussion based on documents from first degree, in particular Transcript of Records) (15 %), 

3. Analytical skills (20 %), 

4. Interpretation skills (20 %), 

5. Ability to explore various aspects of an issue (20 %). 

## **Section 3 Scope and Structure of the Master's Degree Programme, Examinations, Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1) Scope and structure of the Master's degree programme in The Americas/Las Américas as well as the scope and type of examinations are set forth in the **Appendix** . 

(2)[1] Contrary to Section 5 (5) **ABMStPO/Phil** , the teaching and examination languages in the Master's degree programme in The Americas/Las Américas are English and Spanish.[2] Individual compulsory elective modules are offered in German, but options in English and Spanish shall always be offered as an alternative.[3] Further details are stipulated in the module handbook.[4] In any case of uncertainty, the examination language shall be the same as the teaching language. 

## **Section 4 Legal Validity and Transitory Provisions**
