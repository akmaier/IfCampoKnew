## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree programme and examination regulations for the research-orientated Master's degree programme English Studies leading to the degree 'Master of Arts (M.A.)' supplement the valid version of the General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's Degree Programmes at the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology at FAU – **ABMStPO/Phil** dated 27 September 2007. 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master’s Degree Programme, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] A subject-specific degree within the meaning of Section 35 (1)(1) **ABMStPO/Phil** shall be a degree in a two-subject Bachelor’s degree programme in English and/or American Studies.[2] Subject-related degrees or degrees considered to be not significantly different within the meaning of Section 35 (1) no.1 in conjunction with paragraph 2 **ABMStPO/Phil** shall be degrees from the fields of linguistics, literary or cultural studies, provided subject-specific knowledge accounting for at least 60 ECTS credits was obtained in one or more of these areas, with a focus on the areas English studies and /or American studies. 

(2) The following further documents shall be included with the application within the meaning of **(2)(2)(2) Appendix 1 ABMStPO/Phil** : 

1. A CV in tabular form, 

2. Evidence of proficiency in English at a level equivalent to at least C1 of the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) for languages or equivalent evidence according to the table of equivalence published by the Language Centre at FAU, and 3. In the case of paragraph 1, sentence 2 an academic paper written by the student in English during their first degree programme (e.g. assignment/seminar paper or a chapter from the thesis) of between 5 and 20 pages in length providing evidence of advanced knowledge in at least one of the areas relating to English studies or American studies. 

(3)[1] The decision as to whether the degree can be classed as a subject-related degree or a degree which is not significantly different in terms of qualification shall be taken on the basis of the Transcript of Records and the academic paper pursuant to (2) (3). 2For applicants with an overall grade on their subject-specific degree or, in the case of Section 35 (4) **ABMStPO/Phil** , an average of their achievements hitherto between 2.51 and 3.30, a selection interview shall be held; the remaining applicants shall not be admitted to the second level of the qualification assessment process and shall receive a rejection notification including reasons.[3] In the selection interview, which is held in English, applicants shall be assessed according to the following criteria, which are weighted as specified: 

1. Quality of academic knowledge of literary and cultural studies with relation to Great Britain and/or linguistics and applied linguistics, especially with relation to the English language (30%) 

2. Ability to apply methods used in the humanities and social sciences, analyse texts in a broad sense, show understanding of linguistic issues and cultural processes, and identify and analyse cultural practices and representations (40%) 

Quality of English skills and knowledge of the English language (30%).

## **Section 3 Scope and Structure of the Master's Degree Programme, Examinations, Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1)[1] The degree programme is offered with or without a specialisation and comprises modules worth 120 ECTS credits.[2] It can be studied in one of two specialisations ( _Culture and Literature_ or _Linguistics and Applied Linguistics_ ) that students can choose at the beginning of their studies.[3] If no specialisation is chosen, a minimum of 30 ECTS credits must be completed from _Linguistics and Applied Linguistics_ and a minimum of 30 ECTS credits must be completed from _Culture and Literature_ .[4] The specialisations and elective modules in the Master's degree programme in English Studies as well as the type and scope of the examinations shall be set forth in the **Appendices 1** to **6** . 

(2)[1] The teaching and examination language of the Master's degree programme in English Studies is English.[2] Individual courses and examinations may be held in a different language (in particular German); further details are set out in the **Appendices** and the module handbook.[3] In case of doubt the examination language shall be the same as the teaching language. 

## **Section 4 Calculation of Final Grade** 

> 1In the specialisation _Culture and Literature_ , the module _Wild Card_ , _Academic Language Skills modules_ , the _elective module_ and an additional module from the first year of study chosen by the student shall be disregarded in the calculation of the final grade. 

> 2In the specialisation _Linguistics and Applied Linguistics_ the final grade shall be calculated based on all mandatory modules, with two modules from the first year of study to be chosen by the student being disregarded in the calculation of the final grade.[3] For students without a specialisation, the final grade shall be calculated using all modules except for the module _Wild Card_ and two further modules from the first year of study to be chosen by the student.[ 4] Students must notify the Examinations Office of the modules that they have chosen to be disregarded at the latest after the grade of the last examination achievement is announced; if no choice is submitted, the worst module grade or the two worst module grades shall be disregarded. 

## **Section 5 Legal Validity and Transitory Provisions** 

(1) These degree programme and examination regulations shall come into effect on the day after their publication. 

(2)[1] The fifth amendment statute of 7 March 2017 shall come into effect on the day after its publication.[2] It shall apply to all students starting a degree programme from the winter semester 2017/2018 onwards. 

(3)[1] The sixth amendment statute shall come into effect on 1 October 2017.[2] It shall apply to all students starting a degree programme from the winter semester 2017/2018 onwards.
