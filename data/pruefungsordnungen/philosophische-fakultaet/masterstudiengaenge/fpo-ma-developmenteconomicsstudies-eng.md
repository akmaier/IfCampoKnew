## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree programme and examination regulations for the research-oriented consecutive Master's degree programme 'Development Economics and International Studies' with the title 'Master of Arts (MA)' complement the General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's Degree Programmes at the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology of Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg – ABMStPO/Phil. 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master's Degree Programme, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] Subject-specific degrees are Bachelor's degree programmes with economicsrelated content worth at least 70 ECTS credits.[2] Degrees from economics degree programmes with at least 50 ECTS credits in economics in particular shall be recognised as subject-related degrees within the meaning of Section 35 (1)(1) in conjunction with Paragraph 2 ABMStPO/Phil. 

1 

(2)[1] The application documents shall contain proof of English language proficiency of at least level C1 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages. 2The requirement of German language proficiency according to Section 4 (5)(14) of the enrolment statutes for applicants with a first language other than German may be deviated from if proper participation in the degree programme is otherwise ensured, e.g. through enrolment in English-language modules and evidence of the necessary language proficiency. 

(3)[ 1] For applicants with an overall grade on their degree or, in the case of Section 35 (4) ABMStPO/Phil, an average of their achievements hitherto between 2.51 and 2.75, a selection interview shall be held; the remaining applicants shall not be admitted to the second level of the qualification assessment process and shall receive a rejection notification including reasons.[2] The selection interview shall demonstrate that the applicant possesses the required technical and methodological expertise and can be expected to carry out independent academic work in a more research-oriented degree programme.[3] In the selection interview, applicants shall be evaluated according to the following criteria in particular, which are weighted as specified: 

1. Quality of knowledge of microeconomics (one third) 

2. Quality of knowledge of macroeconomics (one third) 

3. Quality of knowledge of statistics (one third)
