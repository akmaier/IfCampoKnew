## **Degree Programme and Examination Regulations for the Master's Degree Programme The Americas/Las Américas at the** 

**Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology of the University of Erlangen-Nürnberg – FPOAm – Dated 08 June 2010** 

amended by statutes of 05 November 2010 17 January 2011 03 November 2011 13 June 2014 

Based on Section 13 (1)(2), Section 43 (5)(2), Section 61 (2)(1) of the Bavarian Higher Education Act (Bayerisches Hochschulgesetz, BayHSchG), the University of Erlangen-Nürnberg enacts the following examination regulations: 

TOC 

## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree programme and examination regulations for the research-orientated consecutive Master's degree programme The Americas/Las Américas with the title 'Master of Arts (MA)' complement the valid version of the General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's Degree Programmes at the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology of Friedrich-Alexander-Universität ErlangenNürnberg – ABMStPO/Phil. 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master's Degree Programme, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] Subject-specific degrees are degrees in two-subject Bachelor's degree programmes in the subjects American studies or English/American studies and/or Hispanic studies or Latin American studies.[2] Subject-related degrees within the meaning of Section 34 (1)(1) in conjunction with Paragraph 2 ABMStPO/Phil are defined as one-subject Bachelor's degrees in English/American studies or Latin American/Hispanic studies, three-subject Bachelor's degrees covering content related to English/American studies and/or Latin American/Hispanic studies worth at least 60 ECTS credits, Diplom or Magister degrees in English/American studies and/or Latin American/Hispanic studies, and the first State Examination in English and/or Spanish. 

(2) The application documents shall contain proof of English language proficiency on at least level C1 and Spanish at level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages or vice versa. 

1 

(3)[1] Applicants with an overall grade on their subject-specific degree or, in the case of Section 34 (4) ABMStPO/phil, an average of their achievements hitherto of 2.51 and worse may be admitted to the Master's degree programme The Americas/Las Américas on the basis of a selection interview.[2] The selection interview is supposed to demonstrate that the applicant possesses the required technical and methodological expertise and can be expected to carry out independent academic work in a more research-orientated degree programme.[3] In the selection interview, applicants shall be evaluated according to the following criteria in particular, which are weighted as specified: 

1. Quality of basic knowledge of North American studies and Latin American studies (50 percent). 

2. A positive prognosis based on improving progress during their course of studies (50 percent).

Scope and structure of the Master's degree programme The Americas/Las Américas as well as scope and type of the examinations shall be set forth in the **Appendix** .
