## **Preamble** 

FAU offers the Elite Master's degree program Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (MA) as part of the Elite Network of Bavaria. 

## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree program and examination regulations for the research-oriented Elite Master’s degree program in Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (MA) leading to the degree Master of Arts (MA) supplement the valid version of the General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor’s and Master’s Degree Programs at the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology at FAU – **ABMStPO/Phil** . 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master’s Degree Program** 

(1) Contrary to Section 36 **ABMStPO/Phil** , those applying for admission to the Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (MA) Master’s degree program will be required to: 

1. Hold a university degree (Bachelor’s, Diploma, Magister, Master’s or State Examination) with an above-average grade, in particular in the fields of philology, social sciences, history, education, philosophy, religious studies, anthropology, economics, law, psychology or medicine with a specific academic or research-oriented emphasis or an equivalent degree in the same fields awarded by an institute of higher 

1 

education in Germany or abroad, insofar as the academic or research-oriented nature of the degree does not deviate significantly from the specified requirements for admission 

2. Pass the qualification assessment process pursuant to **Appendix 2** . 

(2)[1] Applicants must belong to the best 50 percent of their class or have completed the degree program in question with a final grade of at least 2.00 (“gut”; good).[2] Section 16 (3) **ABMStPO/Phil** shall apply accordingly. 

(3)[1] If, during the procedure specified in Section 1 (1)(2), the qualifications held by the applicant as required in Section 1 (1)(1) are found to be such that any differences to requirements are reconcilable, the Examinations Committee may grant admission on the condition that proof of further achievements to be specified by the committee and equivalent to a maximum of 20 ECTS credits is submitted within one year of admission to the Master’s program.[2] Admission is granted provisionally.[ 3] Students who fail to provide proof of meeting the conditions within the deadline are de-registered. 

(4)[1] Notwithstanding paragraph 1(1), students enrolled in a relevant Bachelor’s degree program may be admitted to a Master’s degree program if they have achieved at least 140 ECTS credits.[2] Proof of the successfully completed Bachelor’s degree shall be submitted at the latest within one year of taking up the degree program; completing the Bachelor’s degree program is a prerequisite for formally starting the Master’s degree program.[3] Admission to the Master’s degree program is granted provisionally; if proof is not submitted within the deadline, the student shall be de-registered. 

## **Section 3 Scope and Structure of the Master’s Degree Program, Examinations, Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1) The scope and structure of the Elite Master’s degree program Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (MA) and the scope and weighting for examinations are specified in **Appendix 1** . 

(2)[1] The Elite Master’s degree program consists of two phases.[2] The emphasis during the first two semesters is on theoretical content.[3] The next two semesters concentrate on gathering practical intercultural experience with a strong focus on China (3rd semester: obligatory stay at one of FAU’s national or international partner universities or a stay abroad organized independently by the student) and looking at the theoretical foundations in more depth (4th semester: writing Master's thesis). 

(3)[1] The language of teaching and examination in the Elite Master’s degree program “Standards of Decision- Making Across Cultures” is English.[2] Certain lectures and examinations in elective modules may be held in German and/or Chinese; the details are set out in the module handbook.[3] In any case of uncertainty, the examination language shall be the same as the teaching language.

(2) In addition, the Degree Program Committee is responsible for verifying compliance with the qualifications and admission requirements as set out in (2) in conjunction with **Appendix 2** . 

## **Section 5 Resit Examinations** 

> 1Contrary to Section 40 (1) **ABMStPO/Phil** , it is only possible to resit the examinations of the Elite Master’s degree program once.[2] Resit examinations for the modules to be taken abroad (modules 7 to 9) may also be taken in Erlangen.

(1)[1] These degree program and examination regulations shall come into effect on October 1, 2024.[2] They shall apply to all students starting a degree program from winter semester 2024/2025 onwards.

## **Appendix 2: Qualification assessment process** 

(1) The qualification assessment process will be carried out once per year before the start of the lecture period in the winter semester. 

(2) The following documents must be supplied with applications for admission to the Master’s degree program: 

1. Application for admission 

2. Documentation demonstrating that the applicant holds a university degree as specified in Section 2 (1)(1) (degree certificate, transcript of records, diploma supplement or comparable documents) or a transcript of records in the case of Section 2 (4) 

3. Proof of English language skills equivalent at least to Level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) for languages or equivalent proof of English language skills if the university entrance qualification or the relevant initial professional qualification was not acquired in English (for example school leaving certificate stating level of English proficiency, proof of attaining UNIcert examination English II) 

4. CV in tabular form, including a photo 

5. Other qualifications relevant to the degree program such as 

   - a) Proof of completed modules in scientific or research-related studies, for example in qualitative research methods (worth at least 6 ECTS credits) or 

   - b) Proof of completed modules in an explicitly cross-cultural topic or modules with an intercultural and transcultural focus (worth at least 6 ECTS credits) 

   - c) Proof of completing work experience in a non-Western society (at least 3 months fulltime). 

(3)[1] Applications for the qualification assessment process must be submitted via the FAU application portal during a time period published on the degree program’s website pursuant to sentence 2.[2] Applications for the winter semester can be submitted between February 15 and May 31 each year or from April 15 until July 15 each year.[3] The start and end dates for the application period specified in sentence 2 may also be combined in other ways; selecting different start and end dates is not possible. 

(4)[1] In accordance with Section 4, the qualification assessment shall be the responsibility of the Examinations Committee for the Master’s degree program.[2] The Examinations Committee may transfer the task of coordinating and carrying out the process to individual members unless otherwise specified. 

(5)[1] Admission to the qualification assessment process shall be subject to the timely and complete submission of the documents listed in paragraph (2).[2] The qualification assessment process shall be carried out with those applicants who fulfill the requirements according to the provisions in (6).[3] Applicants who are rejected shall receive a rejection notification including reasons and information on legal remedies available. 

(6)[1] The Admissions Committee shall carry out an initial selection based on the submitted documents as part of the qualification assessment process to assess whether an applicant qualifies for a Master’s degree program.[2] Applicants holding the qualifications specified in Section 2 (1)(1) or, in cases in which the provisions of Section 2 (4) apply, who have an average grade to date of 2.00 (= good) or better and applicants who are among the top 50% of their intake will be invited to an interview of approx. 30 minutes that can also take the form of a video call; Section 16 (4) **ABMStPO/Phil** shall apply accordingly for qualifications with a differing grading system.[3] Applicants holding the qualifications specified in Section 2 (1)(1) or, in cases in which the provisions of Section 2 (4) apply, with an average grade to date of 2.01 to 2.50 (= good) or better and/or applicants who belong to the top 

6 

60% of their intake will be invited to participate in a selection interview if they can furnish proof of qualifications of relevance to the Elite Master’s degree program as specified in Section 2 (5).[4] All remaining applicants shall receive a notification of rejection; this will specify the reasons for rejection and provide information on available legal remedies.[ 5] The qualification assessment interview shall be conducted by two members of the Examinations Committee.[6] Applicants must present and, on request, justify their qualifications during the selection interview and provide adequate answers to questions on topics of relevance to the Elite Master’s degree program.[7] The suitability of applicants for admission to the Elite Master’s degree program shall be assessed on the basis of the following criteria (weighting accorded to criteria in brackets): 

1. Knowledge of transcultural issues and awareness of difficulties in interdisciplinary academic contexts (40%) 

2. Candidate’s abstract thinking skills and ability to reflect on intercultural decision-making on the basis of specific examples (30%) 

3. Quality of articulation and communication skills (30%). 

- 8The selection interview shall be rated as “bestanden” (pass) or “nicht bestanden” (fail). 9Records shall be kept of the selection interview. 

(7)[1] The qualification assessment process shall be considered passed if the selection interview is passed; otherwise it shall be considered failed.[2] Applicants will be notified of the result in writing.[3] A rejection notification must include reasons and information on the legal remedies available.[4] It will not be possible to repeat the qualification assessment process on the basis of the documentation submitted with the first application. 

(8) Applicants shall bear their own costs incurred as a result of taking part in the qualification assessment process. 

(9) Confirmation of passing the qualification assessment process shall remain valid indefinitely, provided the Master’s degree program has not been changed significantly.
