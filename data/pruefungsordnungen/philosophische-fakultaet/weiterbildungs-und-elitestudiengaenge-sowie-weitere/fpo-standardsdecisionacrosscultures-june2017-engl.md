## **Degree Programme and Examination Regulations for the Elite Master’s degree programme ‘Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (SDAC) of the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology of Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg - FPOSDAC -** 

## **Dated 14 June 2017** 

Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) has issued the following degree programme and examination regulations in accordance with Section 13 (1)(2), Section. 43 (5) (2) and Section 61 (2)(1) BayHSchG. 

## **Preamble** 

Within the Elite Network of Bavaria, Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) offers the Elite Master’s degree programme ‘Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (SDAC) – Knowledge, Authenticity, Time’ in cooperation with the European Center for Chinese Studies (ECCS) at Beijing University. 

## **Section 1 Scope** 

These degree programme and examination regulations for the research-orientated Elite Master's degree programme ‘Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (SDAC) – Knowledge, Authenticity, Time’ with the degree of 'Master of Arts (MA)' supplement the current version of the General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's Degree Programmes of the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology of Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg – **ABMStPO/Phil** – dated 27 September 2007. 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master’s degree programme** 

(1) Contrary to Section 35 **ABMStPO/Phil** , those applying for admission to the ‘Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (SDAC) – Knowledge, Authenticity, Time’ Master’s degree programme will be required to: 

1. Hold a university degree (Bachelor, Diploma, Magister, Master or Staatsexamen) with an above-average grade, in particular in the fields of philology, social sciences, history, education, philosophy, religious studies, anthropology, economics, law, psychology or medicine with a specific academic or research-orientated emphasis or an equivalent degree in the same fields awarded by an institute of higher education in Germany or abroad, insofar as the academic or research-orientated nature of the degree does not deviate significantly from the specified requirements for admission 

2. Successfully complete the qualification assessment process as described in **Appendix 2** . 

(2)[1] Applicants must be among the top 50% of their intake or must have been awarded a grade of at least 2.00 (‘good’).[2] Section 15 (3) **ABMStPO/Phil** applies accordingly. 

1 

(3)[1] If, during the procedure specified in Section 1 (2)(2), the qualifications held by the applicant as required in Section 1 (1)(1) are found to be such that any differences to requirements are reconcilable, the Examinations Committee may grant admission on the condition that proof of further achievements to be specified by the committee and equivalent to a maximum of 20 ECTS credits is submitted within one year of admission to the Master's programme.[2] Admission in this case shall be granted conditionally. 

(4)[1] Paragraph 1 sentence 1 notwithstanding, students enrolled in a Bachelor's degree programme may be admitted to the Master's degree programme if they have acquired at least 140 ECTS credits.[2] Proof of successful completion of a Bachelor's degree programme must be submitted at the latest within one year of admission to the Master’s degree programme; the award of a Bachelor’s degree is considered to be a formal requirement for admission to the Master’s degree programme.[3] Admission in this case shall be granted conditionally. 

## **Section 3 Scope and structure of the Master's degree programme, teaching and examination language** 

(1) The scope and structure of the Elite Master’s degree programme ‘Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (SDAC) – Knowledge, Authenticity, Time’ and the scope of and weighting afforded to examinations are specified in **Appendix 1** . 

(2)[1] The Master’s degree programme consists of two phases.[2] The emphasis during the first two semesters is on theoretical content.[3] The following two semesters are designed to enable participants to obtain practical experience (mandatory stay at ECCS in Beijing during the third semester) and consolidate their theoretical knowledge acquired during the previous semesters (the Master’s thesis is to be written in the fourth semester). 

(3)[1] The language of teaching and examination in the Master’s degree programme ‘Standards of Decision- Making Across Cultures (SDAC) – Knowledge, Authenticity, Time’ is English.[2] Certain lectures and examinations in elective modules may be held in German and/or Chinese; the details are set out in the module handbook.[3] In any case of uncertainty, the examination language shall be the same as the teaching language. 

## **Section 4 Examinations Committee** 

(1)[1] Contrary to Sections 11 (1) and (14) **ABMStPO/Phil,** a dedicated Examinations Committee will be tasked with organising and implementing the examinations for the Elite Master’s degree programme ‘Standards of Decision-Making Across Cultures (SDAC) – Knowledge, Authenticity, Time’.[2] It will consist of five members.[3] The Professor of Cultural and Social Anthropology, the Junior Professor of Anthropology and the degree programme coordinator shall be members of the Examinations Committee together with the two speakers for the degree programme.[4] The members shall be appointed by the Faculty Council of the Faculty of Humanities, Social Sciences, and Theology.[5] The Examinations Committee shall elect one of the speakers for the degree programme as the chairperson and appoint a deputy in the event of the chairperson’s absence.[6] The term of office of the members shall be five years.[7] It is permissible to reappoint members and reelect the chairperson.[8] The chairperson may assign tasks that are their responsibility to a member of the Examinations Committee. 

2 

(2) In addition, the Examinations Committee is responsible for verifying compliance with the qualifications and admission requirements as set out in Section 2 and **Appendix 2** . 

## **Section 5 Resit examinations** 

> 1Contrary to Section 39 (1) **ABMStPO/Phil** , it is only possible to resit the examinations of the Elite Master’s degree programme once.[2] To ensure compliance with the deadline for resitting examinations specified in Section 39 (2) in conjunction with Section 34 (1)(3) 

**ABMStPO/Phil** , it will also be possible to resit examinations for the modules to be completed in Beijing (modules 7 to 9) in Erlangen. 

## **Section 6 Legal validity and transitional provisions** 

These degree programme and examination regulations shall come into effect on the day after their publication.

## **Appendix 2: Qualification assessment process** 

(1) The qualification assessment process will be carried out once per year before the start of the lecture period in the winter semester. 

- (2)[1] The following documents must be supplied with applications for admission to the Master's degree programme: 

1. Completed admission application form 

2. Documentation demonstrating that the applicant holds a university degree as specified in Section 2 (1)(1) (degree certificate, transcript of records, diploma supplement or comparable documents) or a transcript of records if there is application for admission under the circumstances described in Section 2 (4) 

3. Proof of English language skills equivalent at least to Level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference (CER) for languages or equivalent proof of English language skills if the higher education entrance qualification or the relevant initial professional qualification was not acquired in the English language 

4. A CV in tabular form including a passport photograph 

5. Other qualifications of relevance to the degree programme; e.g. proof of successful completion of modules relating to academic or research achievements (at least 6 ECTS credits), proof of successful completion of modules of an expressly cross-cultural nature (at least 6 ECTS credits) and/or proof of professional experience obtained in a nonWestern country (at least 3 months full-time) or equivalent evidence. 

2Proof that the applicant has the English language skills specified in Sentence 1 (3)(2) can be provided in the form of the German higher education entrance qualification (Abitur) or comparable documents evidencing skills at level UNIcert C2 or CER B2. 

(3)[1] Applications must be submitted to the university’s Master’s Office at the latest by 15 July of a given year for the following winter semester.[2] The Examinations Committee may grant an extension of this deadline on request. 

(4)[1] In accordance with Section 4, the qualification assessment process shall be undertaken by the Examinations Committee.[2] The Examinations Committee may transfer the task of coordinating and carrying out the process to individual members unless otherwise specified. 

(5)[1] Admission to the qualification assessment process shall be subject to the timely and complete submission of the documents listed in Section 2.[2] The qualification assessment process will be undertaken for applications submitted by applicants who meet the requirements specified in Section 6.[3] Applicants who are rejected will receive a notification of rejection; this will specify the reasons for rejection and provide information on available legal remedies. 

(6)[1] The Examinations Committee will preselect candidates on the basis of the submitted documentation during the qualification assessment process on the basis of whether they are suitable in general for admission to a Master's degree programme.[2] Applicants holding the qualifications specified in Section 2 (1) (1) or, in cases in which the provisions of Section 2 (4) apply, who have an average grade to date of 2.00 (= good) or better and applicants who are among the top 50% of their intake will be invited to an interview of approx. 30 minutes that can also take the form of a video call; Section 15 (4) **ABMStPO/Phil** applies accordingly to qualifications for which a different grading system has been employed.[3] Applicants holding the qualifications specified in Section 2 (1) (1) or, in cases in which the provisions of Section 2 (4) apply, with an average grade to date of 

6 

2.01 to 2.50 (= good) or better and/or applicants who belong to the top 60% of their intake will be invited to participate in a selection interview if they can furnish proof of qualifications of relevance to the Elite Master’s degree programme as specified in Section 2 (5).[4] The remaining applicants will receive a notification of rejection; this will specify the reasons for rejection and provide information on available legal remedies.[5] The selection interview will be carried out by at least two members of the Examinations Committee.[6] The Examinations Committee can request university lecturers whose qualifications correspond with the applicant’s profile to take part in the interview in an advisory capacity (without the right to vote).[7] Applicants must present and, on request, justify their qualifications during the selection interview and provide adequate answers to questions on topics of relevance to the Elite Master’s degree programme.[8] The suitability of applicants for admission to the Elite Master’s degree programme will assessed on the basis of the following criteria (weighting accorded to each criterion in brackets): 

1. Quality of knowledge of cross-cultural issues and awareness of problems involved in interdisciplinary academic research (40%), 

2. Their capacity for abstract thought and their ability to assess specific examples of intercultural decision-making practices (30%), 

3. Their ability to express themselves and communicate (30%). 

9The result of the selection interview will be rated as 'bestanden' (pass) or 'nicht bestanden' (fail).[10] A record of the selection interview will be kept. 

(7)[1] The result of the qualification assessment process will be rated as 'bestanden' (pass) or 'nicht bestanden' (fail); applicants will be notified of the result in writing.[2] Any notification of rejection will specify the reasons for rejection and provide information on available legal remedies.[3] It will not be possible to repeat the qualification assessment process on the basis of the documentation submitted with the first application. 

(8) Applicants shall themselves bear the costs of the qualification assessment process.
