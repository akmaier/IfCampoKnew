## **Part I: General Provisions** 

## **Section 37 Scope** 

The degree programme and examination regulations for the Bachelor’s degree programme in Geosciences (BSc) and the Master’s degree programme in Geosciences (MSc) supplements the current version of the general examination regulations for the Bachelor’s degree programme in Geosciences (BSc) and the Master’s degree programme in Geosciences (MSc) at the Faculty of Sciences at FAU ( **ABMPOGeo/ NatFak** ). 

## **Section 38 Bachelor's Degree Programme, Standard Duration of Study** 

1The Bachelor's degree programme in Geosciences (BSc) shall consist of modules worth 180 ECTS credits distributed over six semesters.[2] This shall include the period for working on the Bachelor's thesis. 

## **Section 39 Master's Degree Programme, Standard Duration of Study,** 

## **Teaching Language** 

(1)[1] The Master's degree programme in Geosciences (MSc) builds on the contents of the Bachelor's degree programme in Geosciences (BSc).[2] It consists of modules worth 120 ECTS credits including the Master's thesis. 

(2)[1] The standard duration of the Master's degree programme is four semesters.[2] The total standard duration for the consecutive Bachelor's and Master's degree programme is ten semesters. 

(3)[1] The teaching and examination language is German.[2] Individual modules may be taught and assessed in English.[3] Further details are stipulated in the module handbook. 

## **Section 40 Examinations Committee** 

1The Examinations Committee for the Bachelor's degree programme in Geosciences (BSc) and the Master's degree programme in Geosciences (MSc) shall be made up of five members from the university lecturers involved in teaching geosciences at the Faculty of Sciences.[2] At least three of the members shall be professors. 

## **Part II: Special Provisions** 

## **1. Bachelor's examination** 

## **Section 41 Structure of the Bachelor's Degree Programme** 

The distribution across the semesters, the type and duration of the examinations in the modules and the required number of ECTS credits are set forth in **Appendix 2** . 

## **Section 42 Grundlagen- und Orientierungsprüfung (GOP)** 

In order to pass the Grundlagen- und Orientierungsprüfung examination, students must pass the modules Foundation of geosciences I, Minerals and rocks, and Chemistry and geoscience methods I with a total of 25 ECTS credits pursuant to **Appendix 2** . 

2 

## **Section 43 Bachelor's Examination** 

1The Bachelor's examination comprises the Bachelor’s thesis module worth 15 ECTS credits, compulsory geosciences modules worth 125 ECTS credits, at least six specialisation modules from elective compulsory subject areas I and II worth a total of 30 ECTS credits plus one module from the core skills area worth 5 ECTS credits and one elective module worth 5 ECTS credits.[2] The Bachelor's examination shall have been passed if all required subject modules have been passed achieving 180 ECTS credits. 

## **Section 43a Elective Modules** 

(1)[1] The first learning outcome of the elective module is to give students the opportunity to explore the theory behind at least one specialisation (raw materials and materials science, chemistry, astronomy, computer science, computers in geosciences, archaeometry, geography and biology).[2] The second learning outcome is methodological, training students in interdisciplinary approaches, extending scientific perspectives to other fields of study and gathering experience in interdisciplinary scientific methods.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice gives students the opportunity to create their own particular profile in view of their future career. 

(2)[1] The type and scope of the examination are dependent on the competencies for the chosen module according to (1) and the module handbook.[2] Possible examination achievements are: written examination (45, 60 or 90 min.), written assignment (10–20 pages), presentation (approx. 15, 30 or 45 min.), essay (5–7 pages), report (2-3 pages).[3] The module catalogue is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice. 

(3) The elective modules generally consist of either one lecture (2 semester hours), one lecture with a tutorial (4 semester hours), a practical (2 to 4 semester hours), a seminar (2 to 4 semester hours) or two seminars (2 semester hours each). 

## **Section 44 Bachelor's Thesis** 

(1) Students are required to have achieved at least 110 ECTS credits and have passed the Grundlagen- und Orientierungsprüfung in order to be allocated a subject for the Bachelor’s thesis. 

(2)[1] 12 ECTS credits shall be awarded for the Bachelor's thesis.[3] After completion the thesis shall be defended during an oral examination, for which 3 ECTS credits are awarded. 

## **Section 45 Bachelor’s Oral Examination** 

1The Bachelor’s thesis shall be defended either with a presentation lasting approximately 15 minutes, in which the Bachelor’s thesis and its findings are presented followed by a discussion lasting approximately 10 minutes, or a poster presentation lasting approximately 15 minutes followed by a discussion lasting approximately 10 minutes.[2] The date of the presentation or the poster presentation held during a geosciences seminar shall be determined by the supervising lecturer either after the student has submitted their Bachelor's thesis or during the final stage of thesis work. 3The date shall usually be between two and four weeks of the date the thesis is submitted; students shall be notified of the date at least two weeks in advance.[4] A grade shall be awarded for the oral examination by the supervisor and at least one other authorised university lecturer pursuant to Section 20 (1) **ABMPOGeo/NatFak** .

## **2. Master's Examination** 

## **Section 46 Admissions Committee for the Master's Degree Programme** 

The admissions committee for the Geosciences (MSc) Master's degree programme shall comprise at least one professor as the chairperson, a university lecturer, and a full-time research associate working for the University. 

## **Section 47 Qualification for a Master’s Degree Programme** 

## **Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] Students shall have completed a degree with above-average grades in the same or a related subject to qualify for the Master's degree programme. 

2Qualification pursuant to sentence 1 shall be proved through: 

1. Final academic record for the Bachelor's examination pursuant to these examination regulations or an equivalent qualification awarded in Germany or abroad, provided the qualifications do not differ significantly from the required qualifications pursuant to (2) 

2. English language proficiency at level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages or equivalent 

3. A pass in the qualification assessment process according to **Appendix 1** . 

(2)[1] A subject-specific degree within the meaning of paragraph 1 (1) is a Bachelor's degree or a Diplom degree in geosciences.[2] Qualifications in a related subject pursuant to paragraph 1 (1) are in particular qualifications from scientific or technical degree programmes which cover geosciences, including an individual specialisation relating to geosciences for which at least 120 ECTS credits have been awarded.[3] If the qualifications pursuant to paragraph 1 (2)(1) are not entirely equivalent but the differences can be compensated, the Admissions Committee may grant admission under the condition that proof of further achievements worth up to a maximum of 20 ECTS credits and to be determined by the Admissions Committee be submitted within one year of starting the Master's degree. 

(3)[1] Paragraph 1 (1) notwithstanding, students enrolled in a Bachelor's degree programme may be admitted to a Master's degree programme in exceptional circumstances if they have achieved at least 140 ECTS credits and make a request stating reasons.[2] Proof of the successfully completed Bachelor's degree shall be submitted at the latest within one year of beginning the degree programme; completing the Bachelor's degree programme is a prerequisite for formally starting the Master's degree.[3] Admission to the Master's degree programme shall be granted conditionally. 

## **Section 48 Scope and Structure of the Master's Degree Programme** 

(1)[1] The Master's examination shall consist of the required module examinations including the Master's thesis module pursuant to **Appendix 4** .[2] To complete the Master's degree, students must pass the following module examinations including the Master's thesis module, amounting to a total of 120 ECTS credits: 

1. Modules worth 45 ECTS credits in the first specialisation 

2. Modules worth 30 ECTS credits in the second specialisation 

3. Module worth 5 ECTS credits as elective minor subject 

4. Modules worth 10 ECTS credits from the core skills 

5. The Master's thesis and oral examination (30 ECTS credits). 

4 

(2) Modules that were the subject of a Bachelor's examination may not as a rule count towards the Master's examination. 

## **Section 48a Elective Minor Subject Module** 

(1)[1] The learning outcome of the elective minor subject is to give students the opportunity to explore the theory of at least one specialist field (material and materials science, computer science, geography and biology).[2] The second learning outcome is methodological, training students in interdisciplinary approaches, extending scientific perspectives to other fields of study and gathering experience in interdisciplinary scientific methods.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice gives students the opportunity to create their own particular profile in view of their future career. 

(2)[1] The type and scope of the examination are dependent on the competencies for the chosen module according to (1) and the module handbook.[2] Possible examination achievements are: written examination (45, 60 or 90 min.), written assignment (10–20 pages), presentation (approx. 15, 30 or 45 min.), essay (5–7 pages), report (2–3 pages).[3] The module catalogue is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice. 

(3) The elective modules generally consist of either one lecture (2 semester hours), one lecture with a tutorial (4 semester hours), practical (2 to 4 semester hours), a seminar (2 to 4 semester hours) or two seminars (2 semester hours each). 

## **Section 49 Master's Degree Examinations** 

The distribution across the semesters, the type and duration of the examinations in the Master's degree programme and the required number of ECTS credits are stipulated in **Appendix 4** . 

## **Section 50 Master's Thesis** 

(1) Students are required to have achieved at least 50 ECTS credits in order to be allocated a subject for the Master's thesis. 

(2)[1] 25 ECTS credits shall be awarded for the Master's thesis.[2] After completion it shall be defended during an oral examination, for which 5 ECTS credits are allocated. 

(3)[1] Full-time university lecturers employed at the Faculty of Sciences and involved in teaching geosciences at GeoZentrum Nordbayern shall be authorised to assign Master's theses.[2] The Examinations Committee shall have the right to grant exceptions. 

(4)[1] The time between the selection of a subject and the submission of the Master's thesis shall be six months (standard thesis work period); the scope of the subject must be such that it can be dealt with within this period.[2] The Examinations Committee shall have the right to extend the period for the Master's thesis by a maximum of three months upon receiving a justified request.[3] If a student submits a doctor's certificate proving that they are incapable of working on the Master's thesis, the period for thesis work shall be suspended temporarily. 

## **Section 51 Master’s Oral Examination** 

1The oral examination in the Master’s degree programme comprises a presentation of approximately 20 minutes, in which the Master's thesis and its findings are presented in a public seminar, followed by a discussion lasting approximately 10 

5 

minutes.[2] The date of the presentation shall be determined by the supervising lecturer either after the student has submitted their Master's thesis or during the final stage of thesis work.[3] The date shall usually be within four weeks after the date on which the thesis was submitted; students shall be notified of the date at least two weeks in advance.[4] A grade shall be awarded for the presentation and the discussion by the supervisor and at least one other authorised university lecturer pursuant to Section 20 (1) **ABMPOGeo/NatFak** . 

## **Part III: Final Provisions** 

## **Section 52 Legal Validity** 

1These degree programme and examination regulations shall come into effect on the day after their publication.[2] They shall apply to all students who enter the Bachelor’s degree programme Geosciences (BSc) or the Master's degree programme Geosciences (MSc) in the winter semester 2016/2017 or later.[3] Students who are already studying under the previous examination regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's degree programmes in Geosciences at the Faculty of Sciences at FAU from 1 October 2007 in the version dated 18 February 2013 shall sit their examinations according to the previously valid examination regulations.

## **Appendix 1: Qualification Assessment Process** 

(1) The qualification assessment process shall be carried out as necessary but at least once per academic year. 

(2)[1] Applications for the qualification assessment process must be submitted to the University (L4 Master's Office) by 15 July for the winter semester using the given form.[2] The application shall contain: 

1. A certificate proving that the applicant holds a qualification according to Section 47 (1)(2)(1) (final academic record, transcript of records, diploma supplement or comparable documents) or a transcript of records in the case set forth in Section 47 (3) 

2. Proof of English language proficiency at level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages; applicants who completed their university entrance qualification or first degree in English shall not be required to provide proof of proficiency in English 

3. A letter of application including the following: 

   - educational background 

   - extent of training in geosciences 

   - preferred selection of first and second specialisations. 

(3)[1] In accordance with Section 12 **ABMPOGeo/NatFak** , qualification assessment shall be the responsibility of the Admissions Committee.[2] The Admissions Committee may transfer the task of coordinating and carrying out the process to individual members unless otherwise stated.[3] The Admissions Committee shall fulfil its obligations in cooperation with the Master's Office. 

(4)[1] Admission to the qualification assessment process shall be subject to the timely and complete submission of the documents listed in (2).[2] The qualification assessment process shall be carried out with those applicants who fulfil the requirements according to the provisions in (5).[3] Applicants who are rejected shall receive a rejection notification including reasons and information on legal remedies available. 

(5)[1] The Admissions Committee shall shortlist applications based on the submitted documents as part of the qualification assessment process to assess whether an applicant qualifies for a Master's degree programme.[2] The Admissions Committee shall deem the applicant qualified according to the submitted documents if the final grade of the subject-specific degree fulfils the provisions under Section 47 (2)(1), or in the case of Section 47 (3) if the student has been confirmed to have achieved an average of 2.50 or better in their previous subject-specific achievements.[3] Applicants with a final grade for their subject-specific qualification or in the case of Section 47 (3) an average of their previous subject-specific achievements of at worst 3.5 or a final grade for their subject-related qualification or in the case of Section 47 (3) an average of their previous subject-related achievements of between 1.0 and 3.5 shall be invited to a selection interview. All other applicants shall receive a rejection letter stating reasons for the decision and legal remedies available.[4] Section 13 (3) **ABMPOGeo/ NatFak** shall apply accordingly for qualifications with a differing grading system.[5] The date of the selection interview shall be announced at least one week in advance.[6] If an applicant should be unable to attend due to reasons beyond their control, a second date may be set upon justified request up until two weeks before the start of the lecture period.[7] The selection interview is an individual examination lasting approximately 15 minutes.[8] With the applicant's approval, the selection interview may be 

7 

conducted as a video conference.[9] It shall be conducted by two university lecturers from the GeoZentrum Nordbayern appointed by the Admissions Committee. Section 18 (4) **ABMPOGeo/NatFak** shall apply accordingly.[10] The selection interview is intended to demonstrate that the applicant possesses the required technical and methodological expertise and can be expected to carry out independent academic work in a more research-orientated degree programme. It covers in particular the following criteria: 

- sound knowledge of the fundamental concepts relating to geosciences (60 %), including individually selected subject specialisations from the field of geosciences (30 %) 

- a positive prognosis based on improving performance over the course of the studies so far (10 %).
