## **Degree Programme and Examination Regulations for the Master of Science in Computational Engineering of the Bavarian Graduate School** 

# **at the Faculty of Engineering at Friedrich-AlexanderUniversität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) Dated 15 March 2005** 

amended by statutes of 30 July 2013 31 March 2016 

Based on Section 13 (1)(2), Section 43 (5), Section 61 (2)(1) of the Bavarian Higher Education Act (Bayerisches Hochschulgesetz, BayHSchG), Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) enacts the following degree programme and examination regulations: 

## **Preamble:** 

1As part of the Elite Network of Bavaria (ENB), the international Master's degree programmes 'Computational Mechanics' (Technische Universität München), 'Computational Science and Engineering' (Technische Universität München) and 'Computational Engineering' (FAU) form the Bavarian Graduate School of Computational Engineering. 2The Bavarian Graduate School offers an Elite programme in the field of computational engineering, enabling students of the above-mentioned degree programmes to obtain a special Master of Science degree.[3] The degree certificate shall state that the degree was obtained 'with honours', documenting the outstanding achievements of graduates going beyond a standard Master's degree. 

## **Section 1** 

## **Scope** 

1These degree programme and examination regulations complement the current version of the degree programme and examination regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's degree programmes in Computational Engineering at the Faculty of Engineering at FAU from 13 April 2000 (KWMBI II, p. 940).[2] They govern, in particular: 

1. The admission requirements for the Master's degree programme in the Elite network programme 

2. The scope of the additional examination 

3. The examination periods and resit examinations 

4. The appointed bodies 

5. The degree title. 

1 

## **Section 2 Degree Title** 

The degree 'Master of Science' shall be awarded to students within the Elite Network programme who have passed the Master's examination according to the degree programme and examination regulations mentioned in Section 1 and the additional examination according to these examination regulations with a minimum grade average of 2.0. 

## **Section 3** 

## **ENB Computational Engineering Committee, Examinations Committees** 

(1)[1] The ENB Computational Engineering Committee shall consist of two full-time professors from FAU and four full-time professors from Technische Universität München. 2Two members each shall be appointed by the School Councils of the Department of Civil, Geo, and Environmental Engineering and the Department of Informatics at Technische Universität München and the Faculty of Engineering at FAU.[3] The term of office shall be two years.[4] The members of the Committee shall elect a speaker by secret ballot. 

(2) The ENB Computational Engineering Committee shall carry out the qualification assessment process for the Elite programme according to Section 4. 

(3) The examinations committee responsible for the student shall be the examinations committee of the Master's degree in which the student is enrolled. 

## **Section 4** 

## **Qualification Requirements for the Elite Network Master's Degree Programme** 

1Applicants who fulfil the following requirements shall be qualified for the Master's degree programme in Computational Engineering: 

1. The applicant must have an undergraduate degree in computational engineering from a university or a degree in another relevant subject. 

2. The applicant's final grade in this degree must be significantly above average. 

3. The applicant must pass the qualification assessment process according to the **Appendix** . 

2Scientific degree programmes in engineering or the natural sciences which the ENB Committee has recognised as being related to the subject of computational engineering shall be considered relevant within the meaning of sentence 1 (1).[3] The results of the undergraduate degree shall be considered as significantly above average within the meaning of sentence 1 (2) if they are graded at least 'gut' (good; 2.0) or if it is proven that the result is among the top 10 % of the applicant's year. 

## **Section 5** 

## **Scope and Procedure for the Additional Examination, Examination Periods** 

(1) In addition to the Master's examination, which is governed by the relevant degree programme and examination regulations as set forth in Section 1, in order to obtain a 'Master of Science' degree as part of the Elite programme students must complete course and examination achievements worth a minimum of 30 ECTS credits within the context of the additional examination. 

(2) The additional examination shall consist of: 

a) a research project worth 10 ECTS credits. 

2 

- b) 10 ECTS credits' worth of teaching units conveying transferable skills. 

- c) 10 ECTS credits' worth of interdisciplinary, subject-orientated teaching units that are not offered as part of the Master's degree programme in Computational Engineering. 

(3)[1] The research project according to (2)(a) shall consist of a task that shall be carried out in a team and usually involve an industrial company.[2] The research project shall be in the field of computational engineering or a related field.[3] The contributions of individual students to team work shall be clearly identifiable and appraisable. 

(4)[1] The teaching units which can be chosen according to (2)(b) shall have an interdisciplinary character and convey transferable skills such as core skills and soft skills. 2The teaching units according to (2)(c) shall be chosen from the partner programmes Computational Science and Engineering and Computational Mechanics at Technische Universität München.[3] The list of teaching units shall be determined by resolution of the ENB Committee and announced by bulletin on the notice board.[4] The teaching units may also take the form of teleteaching or block, weekend or holiday courses. 

(5)[1] The lecturer responsible for the course shall announce the type of examination (written or oral) and the duration of the examination one week after the start of the lecture period at the latest in accordance with local practice.[2] The duration of written examinations shall be between 60 and 120 minutes.[3] The duration of oral examinations shall be approximately 30 minutes.[4] Every individual examination shall cover material taught in teaching units of no less than two and no more than eight semester hours. 

(6)[1] Students shall register for the examinations of the additional examination in good time so as to be able to sit the examinations within the standard duration of the Master's degree programme.[2] Students who exceed the standard duration by more than one semester due to reasons for which they are responsible shall be excluded from the additional examination. 

## **Section 6** 

## **Evaluation of the Additional Examination, Resits** 

(1)[1] The examination achievements according to Section 5 (2) must have a minimum grade average of 'gut' (good; 2.0); no examination achievement may have a worse grade than 'ausreichend' (sufficient; 4.0).[2] Examinations with a worse grade than 'gut' (good; 2.0) may be resat once; the resit examination shall take place within six months of the original examination. 

(2)[1] The overall grade of the additional examination shall be calculated as a weighted grade average of the examinations according to Section 5 (2).[2] The grade weighting of the individual examination achievements shall correspond to the respective number of ECTS credits. 

## **Section 7** 

## **Final Academic Record and Certificate** 

(1)[1] A final academic record shall be issued for Master's examinations passed successfully in the Elite Programme of the Elite Network of Bavaria; in addition to the details according to Section 15 (1) of the degree programme and examination regulations mentioned in Section 1, it shall list the achievements according to Section 5 (2). 

3 

2The subject and grade of the Master's thesis and the overall grade shall also be included. 

(2) Three quarters of the final grade of the Master's examination in the Elite Programme shall be made up of the overall grade according to Section 15 (1)(2) of the degree programme and examination regulations mentioned in Section 1. The additional examination worth 30 ECTS credits according to Section 5 (2) shall account for one quarter of the final grade. 

(3)[1] If the requirements in Section 2 are fulfilled, a Master of Science degree shall be conferred.[2] Graduates shall be issued with a degree certificate conferring them the title Master of Science with honours. 

(4) Students who have passed the Master's examination and the examination achievements according to Section 5 (2) with a minimum grade of 'ausreichend' (sufficient) without achieving the minimum grade average of 2.0 ('gut'/good) required for the addition 'with honours' shall receive a certificate confirming the achievements obtained in the programme of the Elite Network according to Section 5 (2) in addition to the Master's degree according to Section 2 (2) of the degree programme and examination regulations mentioned in Section 1. 

## **Section 8 Legal Validity** 

These degree programme and examination regulations shall come into effect on the day after their publication.

## **Appendix: Qualification Assessment Process** 

## **1. Qualification Assessment Process** 

The qualification assessment process is usually carried out by the ENB Computational Engineering Committee once per semester. 

## **2. Admission** 

2.1 Applications for admission to the qualification assessment process shall be submitted to the chairperson of the Admissions Committee using the forms issued by the ENB Committee by 1 September or 1 March, respectively. 

2.2 The application shall include: 

2.2.1 A CV in tabular form 

2.2.2 Documents demonstrating fulfilment of admission requirements according to Section 4 

2.2.3 Letter of a maximum length of two A4 pages stating reasons for choosing the Elite Programme Computational Engineering at FAU; the applicant shall state the aptitudes and interests making them especially qualified for the Elite Programme in Computational Engineering at FAU. 

2.2.4 Letters of recommendation from two university lecturers from previous studies 2.3 Applicants who obtained their Bachelor's or Diplom degree from FAU shall not be required to submit the documents in 2.2.2. 

## **3. Qualification Assessment Committee** 

The qualification assessment process shall be carried out by the ENB Computational Engineering Committee. 

## **4. Admission to the Qualification Assessment Process** 

4.1 Admission to the qualification assessment process shall be subject to the timely and complete submission of the documents listed in No. 2.2.2. 

4.2 An assessment interview shall be conducted according to No. 5 with applicants who fulfil the requirements. 

4.3 Applicants who are rejected shall receive a rejection notification including reasons and information on legal remedies available. 

## **5. Stages in the Qualification Assessment Process** 

5.1[1] The date of the qualification assessment interview shall be announced at least one week in advance.[2] If an applicant should be unable to attend the qualification assessment interview due to reasons beyond their control, a second date may be set upon justified request up until two weeks before the start of the lecture period. 

5.2[1] The qualification assessment qualification assessment process shall be held individually for each applicant.[2] The interview shall have a duration of between 20 and 30 minutes per applicant and is intended to determine whether the applicant can be expected to complete the degree programme independently, responsibly and by taking an academic approach.[3] The qualification assessment interview shall deal with the applicant's motivation and qualification for the Elite Programme in the field of computational engineering.[4] Subject-specific knowledge that will be taught during the Master's degree programme shall not be decisive in the interview.[5] The applicant shall successfully convey the impression that they are qualified to fulfil the higher requirements of the additional examination. 

5 

5.3[1] The interview shall be conducted by at least two members of the Qualification Assessment Committee.[2] The assessment, which must be unanimous, shall be 'bestanden' (pass) or 'nicht bestanden' (fail). 

5.4[1] Applicants shall be notified of the result of the qualification assessment process in writing by the chairperson of the ENB Committee.[2] A rejection notification shall include reasons and information on the legal remedies available.[3] The qualification assessment process may not be repeated on the basis of the documentation submitted with the first application. 

5.5[1] An official record shall be produced of the qualification assessment process, showing the date, duration and place of the assessment, the names of the Committee members, the names of the applicants, the decision of the Committee members and the overall result.[2] The official records shall clearly indicate the topics discussed with the applicants and the essential reasons for the decision.
