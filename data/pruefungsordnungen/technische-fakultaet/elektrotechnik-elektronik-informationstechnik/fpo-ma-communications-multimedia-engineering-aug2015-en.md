**Degree Programme and Examination Regulations for the Master's Degree Programme in Communications and Multimedia Engineering at the Faculty of Engineering of Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg – FPOCME – Dated 5 August 2011** 

amended by statutes of 14 October 2013 11 August 2015 

Based on Section 13 (1)(2) in conjunction with Section 43 (5) and Section 61 (2) of the Bavarian Higher Education Act (Bayerisches Hochschulgesetz, BayHSchG), FriedrichAlexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) enacts the following examination regulations: 

## **Part I: General Provisions** 

## **Section 34 Scope** 

The degree programme and examination regulations for the Master's degree programme Communications and Multimedia Engineering complement the currently valid General Examination Regulations for the Bachelor's and Master's Degree Programmes at the Faculty of Engineering of Friedrich-Alexander-Universität ErlangenNürnberg – **ABMPO/TechFak** –. 

## **Section 35 Master's Degree Programme, Standard Duration of Study, Start of Degree Programme** 

(1)[1] The Master's degree programme in Communications and Multimedia Engineering is based on Bachelor's and Diploma degree programmes with a focus on information and communication technology.[2] It shall consist of modules worth 120 ECTS credits in total and shall include a Master's thesis with a six-month period for thesis work. 

(2) By default, duration of study shall be four semesters. 

(3) The distribution of the modules over the default duration of the study programme shall be set out in **Appendix 1** and **Appendix 2** . 

(4) The Master's degree programme in Communications and Multimedia Engineering can be started in the winter semester and can be studied as a full-time or part-time degree programme. 

1 

## **Section 36 International Orientation** 

1The Communications and Multimedia Engineering degree programme shall be taught in English.[2] Students may opt for oral examinations to be carried out in either English or German.[3] The Master's thesis is written in English by default.[4] Final academic records shall be issued in German and English. 

## **Part II: Special Provisions** 

## **Section 37 Qualification for a Master's Degree Programme, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] A subject-specific degree as defined by Section 29 (1)(1) **ABMPO/TechFak** shall be a Bachelor's degree in information and communication technology; computational engineering; or electrical engineering, electronics, and information technology.[2] In accordance with (5)(4) of **Appendix 1 ABMPO/TechFak** , applicants with a subject-related degree or an equivalent degree within the meaning of Section 29 (1)(1) **ABMPO/TechFak** shall only be admitted to the Master's degree programme after passing an oral admission examination according to (3). 

(2) The following documents shall be submitted as certificates according to **Appendix 1** , paragraph (2)(4) **ABMPO/TechFak** : 

1. Proof of English-language proficiency. The following shall be accepted as proof of English-language proficiency: Abitur (university entrance qualification) certificate, subject-specific university entrance qualification for engineering (fachgebundene Hochschulreife in Fachrichtung Technik; FOS-13 or SOS) or comparable certificates at the level of UNIcert C2 or Common European Framework of Reference for Languages B2. 

2. An application form completed in English (available on the website or from the Admissions Committee). 

3.  Two letters of recommendation from university lecturers from German or foreign universities or non-university research institutions. 

(3) In the oral admission examination according to **Appendix 1** , paragraph 5 (3) et seq. **ABMPO/TechFak** , applicants shall be evaluated with regard to the following criteria: 

- solid knowledge of the foundations of the subject, especially 

   - engineering mathematics 

- description of a relevant, subject-related project, knowledge of the relevant literature 

- a positive prognosis based on improving performance over the course of studies so far 

- subject knowledge from the qualifications listed above. 

## **Section 38 Scope, Structure, and Examinations of the Master's Degree** 

## **Programme** 

(1) The Master’s degree programme in Communications and Multimedia Engineering shall consist of mandatory modules (35 ECTS credits in total, cf. study plan in **Appendix 1** or **Appendix 2** ), mandatory elective and laboratory course modules (15 ECTS credits in total, 7.5 ECTS credits for laboratory courses, 10 ECTS credits for the research internship and 2.5 ECTS credits for the seminar; cf. catalogue of elective mandatory modules), non-engineering mandatory elective modules such as soft skills (a total of 15 ECTS credits; at least 2.5 of these ECTS credits shall be from language courses on technical English for German-speaking students and all 15 ECTS credits 

2 

shall be from German language courses for students who are not proficient in German), engineering elective modules (15 ECTS credits in total, cf. catalogue of elective compulsory modules) and the Master's thesis (30 ECTS credits). 

(2) Due to the different levels of previous knowledge among foreign students, they shall be permitted to choose alternative modules for the modules of the compulsory module group in **Appendix 1** or **Appendix 2** after individual consultation with a study advisor and with the Examinations Committee's approval. 

(3) Type and scope of the course and examination achievements are set out in **Appendix 1** , **Appendix 2** and the module handbook. 

## **Section 39 Requirements for Admission to the Master's Thesis** 

(1) In order to begin working on the Master's thesis project, students must have passed the mandatory modules specified in **Appendix 1** or **Appendix 2** and have obtained a minimum of 80 ECTS credits. 

(2) In justified, exceptional cases, the Examinations Committee shall be entitled to grant admission to the Master's thesis early, paragraph 1 notwithstanding. 

## **Section 40 Master’s Thesis** 

(1)[1] The Master's thesis is intended to demonstrate students' ability to solve scientific problems in the field of communication and multimedia engineering independently.[2] All full-time university lecturers teaching at the Department of Electrical Engineering shall be entitled to supervise Master’s theses. 

(2) The Master’s thesis shall be worth 30 ECTS credits. 

## **Section 41 Completion and Grading for the Master’s Degree Programme** 

(1) The Master’s degree programme shall have been completed successfully if proof of passing all modules stipulated in **Appendix 1** or **Appendix 2** has been submitted. 

(2)[1] The final grade shall be calculated from the grades of all assessed modules set forth in **Appendix 1** or **Appendix 2** including the Master’s thesis and their ECTS weighting.[2] An interim grade is calculated for each module group and is included with the ECTS weighting of the module group in determining the final grade. 

## **Part III: Final Provisions** 

## **Section 42 Entry into Force** 

1These degree programme and examination regulations shall come into effect on the day after their publication.[2] They shall apply to all students who start the Master's degree programme in Communications and Multimedia Engineering in the winter semester 2011/2012 or later.
