## **Section 1 Scope** 

1These degree programme and examination regulations govern studies and examinations in the Bachelor's degree programme in International Business Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU, leading to the degree Bachelor of Science.[2] They supplement the currently valid version of the general examination regulations for Bachelor's degree programmes at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU  – **BPOWISO** – dated 1 August 2006.

## **Section 3 Interdisciplinary Specialisation Modules** 

(1)[1] The specialisation modules that can be chosen as part of the Bachelor's degree programme in International Business Studies allow students to explore one or several specialist areas in more detail.[2] Students can select modules on the topics of business administration, economics, information systems, socioeconomics, and interdisciplinary subjects. 

(2)[1] The first learning outcome of the specialisation modules in business administration is to allow students to explore in more detail the issue of decision-making in companies, with reference to planning, organisational aspects and accounting.[2] Secondly, the various choices available in the area of business administration offer students a wide range of options for specialisation.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice gives students the opportunity to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies). 

(3)[1] The learning outcome of the specialisation modules from the area of economics is to allow students to gain a deeper insight into issues of economic policy at the level of the state using theoretical and empirical methods as well as analysing the optimal behaviour of individuals and companies in markets.[2] Secondly, the various choices available in the area of economics offer students a wide range of options for specialisation. 3Thirdly, the element of choice gives students the opportunity to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies). 

(4)[1] The learning outcome of the specialisation modules from information systems is to allow students to gain a deeper understanding of the integration of issues relevant to business operations from the fields of business administration and information systems.[2] Secondly, the various choices available in the area of information systems offer students a wide range of options for specialisation[3] Thirdly, the element of choice gives students the opportunity to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies). 

(5)[1] The learning outcome of the specialisation modules in socioeconomics is to allow students to gain deeper understanding of issues relating to the economy and society, as well as to use empirical methods to recognise socioeconomical problems and come up with structured solutions.[2] Secondly, the various choices available in the area of socioeconomics offer students a wide range of options for specialisation.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice gives students the opportunity to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies). 

(6)[1] The learning outcome of the other interdisciplinary specialisation modules is firstly to allow students to explore interdisciplinary topics and areas in more detail and to train students to take an interdisciplinary approach.[2] Secondly, the various choices available in the interdisciplinary specialisation modules offer students a wide range of options 

2 

for specialisation.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice gives students the opportunity to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies). 

(7)[1] The first learning outcome of the stay abroad is to offer students the opportunity to explore interdisciplinary content and topics that are not part of the home university’s main research focus and which therefore cannot be studied in depth at the home university.[2] Secondly, the stay abroad allows students to gain international experience and improve their intercultural skills.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice during the stay abroad offers students a wide range of specialisation options.[4] Fourthly, the wide range of options allows students to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies). 

(8)[1] The type and scope of the examination depend on the skills for the chosen module according to (2) to (6) and the module handbook.[2] The type and scope of the possible examinations are stipulated in Sections 17 to 20a **BPOWISO** .[3] The module catalogue is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice. 4Notwithstanding sentences 1 to 3, the type and scope of the examinations during the semester abroad depend on the regulations in place at the partner university, and learning agreements ought to be concluded before the stay abroad. 

(9)[1] Specialisation modules usually consist of a lecture (4 SWS), a seminar (4 SWS) or a lecture and a tutorial (each 2 SWS).[2] Further provisions shall be set forth in the module handbook. 

## **Section 4 Legal validity** 

(1)[1] These degree programme and examination regulations shall come into effect on 1 October 2017.[2] They shall apply to all students starting a degree programme from winter semester 2017/18 onwards.[3] Notwithstanding sentence 2, the amendment listed in the Appendix regarding the new name of the module ‘International Business’ (formerly ‘Foreign Trade’) shall apply for all students who are already studying under the previously valid degree programme and examination regulations for Bachelor's degree programmes at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU – **BPOWiWi** – dated 15 June 2016 and who have not yet begun that module. 

(2)[1] The first amendment statute shall come into effect on 1 October 2018.[2] It shall apply to all students starting a degree programme from winter semester 2018/2019 onwards. 

(3)[1] The second amendment statute shall come into effect on the day after its publication.[2] Notwithstanding sentence 1, the amendments in the module ‘Strategic and international management I’ in the **Appendix** shall only apply to examination procedures commenced as of winter semester 2019/2020 (first attempt). 

(4)[1] The third amendment statute shall come into effect on the day after its publication. 2It shall apply to all students starting a degree programme from winter semester 2020/2021 onwards.[3] Examinations according to the previously valid examination regulations shall be offered for the last time in summer semester 2024.[4] From the date stated in sentence 3, those students who are affected by the examination regulations becoming invalid shall take their examinations in accordance with the version of the degree programme and examination regulations valid from this date on.

(5)[1] The fourth amendment statute shall come into effect on 1 October 2021.[2] It applies to all students who are already studying in accordance with the valid version of these degree programme and examination regulations as well as those who will commence their studies in winter semester 2021/22.[ 3] Notwithstanding sentence 2, the amendments in the modules ‘Electronic human resources management’, ‘Intercultural competence’, ‘Foreign languages 1.2 and ‘Foreign languages 2’ apply to all students who have not yet started examinations for these modules (first attempt).
