## **Degree Program and Examination Regulations for the Bachelor’s degree program in International Business Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FriedrichAlexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – FPO BSc IBS –** 

**Dated August 7, 2024** 

Based on Section 9 (1) in conjunction with Section 80 (1)(1), Section 84 (2)(1), Section 88 (9) and Section 96 (3)(1) Bavarian Higher Education Innovation Act dated August 5, 2022 ( **BayHIG** ), FAU enacts the following degree program and examination regulations:

## **Section 1 Scope** 

1These degree program and examination regulations govern studies and examinations in the Bachelor's degree program in International Business Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU, leading to the degree Bachelor of Science.[2] They supplement the currently valid version of the general examination regulations for Bachelor's degree programs at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU – **BPOWISO.** 

## **Section 2 Scope and Structure of the Degree Program, Examinations** , **Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1) The Bachelor’s degree program in International Business Studies at FAU is split into modules according to the **Appendix** . 

(2) The type and scope of the examinations are governed by Section 3 and the **Appendix** as well as Sections 16 to 23 of **BPOWISO** . 

(3) Section 3 (5) **BPOWISO** applies with the proviso that the teaching and examination language is English and that individual teaching units and examinations in the (compulsory) elective modules may be held in German or other languages; otherwise, Section 3 (5) **BPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

## **Section 3 Interdisciplinary Specialization Modules (Electives & Electives Abroad)** 

(1)[1] The specialization modules that can be chosen as part of the Bachelor’s degree program in International Business Studies allow students to explore one or several specialist areas in more detail.[2] Students can select modules on the topics of business 

1 

administration, economics, information systems, socioeconomics, and interdisciplinary subjects. 

(2)[1] The learning outcome of the specialization modules in business administration is stipulated in Section 3 (2) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA WiWi** .[2] The type and scope of the examinations are stipulated in Section 3 (6) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA WiWi** . 

(3)[1] The learning outcome of the specialization modules in economics is stipulated in Section 3 (3) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA WiWi** .[2] The type and scope of the examinations are stipulated in Section 3 (6) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA WiWi** . 

(4)[1] The learning outcome of the specialization modules in information systems is stipulated in Section 3 (4) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA WiWi** .[2] Possible examination forms are: written examination, written assignment, case study and presentation as well as combinations of the same; Section 5 (2)(3) **BPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

(5)[1] The learning outcome of the specialization modules in socioeconomics is stipulated in Section 4 (1) and (5) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA SozÖk** .[2] The type and scope of the examinations are stipulated in Section 4 (7) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA SozÖk** . 

(6)[1] The learning outcome of the other interdisciplinary specialization modules is stipulated in Section 4 (1) and (6) of the currently valid version of **FPO BA SozÖk** .[2] The type and scope of the examinations are dependent on the skills taught and are stated in the module handbook.[3] Possible examinations may be: written examination (60 or 90 min), written assignment or seminar paper or a combination of them; Section 5 (2)(3) **BPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

(7)[1] The first learning outcome of the stay abroad is to offer students the opportunity to explore interdisciplinary content and topics that are not part of the home university’s main research focus and which therefore cannot be studied in depth at the home university.[2] Secondly, the stay abroad allows students to gain international experience and improve their intercultural skills.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice during the stay abroad offers students a wide range of specialization options.[4] Fourthly, the wide range of options allows students to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies).[5] In order to achieve the learning outcome of the stay abroad, students must complete it in a country other than the one in which they obtained their university entrance qualification.[6] The type and scope of examinations in modules taken abroad are stipulated in the degree program and examination regulations for the subject or from the host university abroad.[7] The module catalog is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice. 

(8)[1] The module catalog is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice.[2] Notwithstanding sentences 1 to 3, the type and scope of the examinations during the semester abroad depend on the regulations in place at the partner university, and learning agreements ought to be concluded before the stay abroad.

(9)[1] Specialization modules are worth 5 ECTS credits and usually consist of a lecture (4 SWS), a seminar (4 SWS) or a lecture and a tutorial (each 2 SWS).[2] Details are stipulated in the module handbook. 

## **Section 4 Legal Validity** 

(1)[1] These degree program and examination regulations shall come into effect on October 1, 2024.[2] They shall apply to all students starting their degree program as of winter semester 2024/2025 and those students who at the time these regulations come into effect are already studying in accordance with the version dated March 23, 2023 of the degree program and examination regulations for the Master's degree program in International Business Studies at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at Friedrich Alexander Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – FPO BSc IBS – dated August 10, 2017, last amended on March 23, 2023. Students who are studying in accordance with a version of the FPO BSc IBS mentioned in sentence 2 that was valid before March 23, 2023 shall complete their studies in accordance with the degree program and examination regulations stated in sentence 2. 

(2)[1] The degree program and examination regulations for the Bachelor’s degree program in International Business Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – FPO BSc IBS – dated August 10, 2017, last amended on March 23, 2023, shall become invalid as of September 30, 2027.[2] Examinations according to the previously valid degree program and examination regulations stated in sentence 1 shall be offered for the last time in summer semester 2027.[3] From the date stated in sentence 2, those students who are affected by the degree program and examination regulations becoming invalid shall take their examinations in accordance with the version of the degree program and examination regulations valid at this time.
