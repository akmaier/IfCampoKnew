## **Degree Program and Examination Regulations for the Bachelor’s degree program in International Economic Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FriedrichAlexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – FPO BSc IES – Dated August 7, 2024** 

Based on Section 9 (1) in conjunction with Section 80 (1)(1), Section 84 (2)(1), Section 88 (9) and Section 96 (3)(1) Bavarian Higher Education Innovation Act dated August 5, 2022 ( **BayHIG** ), FAU enacts the following degree program and examination regulations:

## **Section 1 Scope** 

1These degree program and examination regulations govern studies and examinations in the Bachelor’s degree program in International Economic Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU, leading to the degree Bachelor of Science.[2] They supplement the currently valid version of the general examination regulations for Bachelor's degree programs at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU – **BPOWISO.** 

## **Section 2 Scope and Structure of the Degree Program, Examinations, Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1) The Bachelor’s degree program in International Economic Studies at FAU is split into modules according to the **Appendix** . 

(2) The type and scope of the examinations are governed by Section 3 and the **Appendix** as well as Sections 16 to 23 of **BPOWISO** . 

(3) Section 3 (5) **BPOWISO** applies with the proviso that the teaching and examination language is English and that individual teaching units and examinations in the (compulsory) elective modules may be held in German or other languages (in particular French and Spanish); otherwise, Section 3 (5) **BPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

## **Section 3 Interdisciplinary Specialization Modules** 

(1)[1] The specialization modules (electives and electives abroad) that can be chosen as part of the Bachelor’s degree program in International Economic Studies allow students to explore one or several specialist areas in more detail.[2] Students can choose modules from the following subject areas: 

1 

- Business administration 

- Economics 

- Information systems 

- Socioeconomics 

- Interdisciplinary subjects and modules taken during the stay abroad.[3] The specific learning outcomes, examination subjects, and the type and scope of examinations depend on the skills taught in the individual modules pursuant to paragraphs 2 to 6 and are stated in the module handbook.[4] The module catalog is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice. 

(2)[1] The overall learning outcome of the specialization modules in business administration is stipulated in Section 3 (2) **FPO BA WiWi** .[2] The type and scope of the examinations are stipulated in Section 3 (6) **FPO BA WiWi** . 

(3)[1] The overall learning outcome of the specialization modules in economics is stipulated in Section 3 (3) **FPO BA WiWi** .[2] The type and scope of the examinations are stipulated in Section 3 (6) **FPO BA WiWi** . 

(4)[1] The overall learning outcome of the specialization modules in information systems is stipulated in Section 3 (4) **FPO BA WiWi** .[2] Possible examination forms are: written examination, written assignment, case study and presentation as well as combinations of the same; Section 5 (2)(3) **BPOWISO** shall apply. 

(5)[1] The overall learning outcome of the specialization modules in socioeconomics is stipulated in Section 4 (1) and (5) **FPO BA SozÖK** .[2] The type and scope of the examinations are stipulated in Section 4 (7) **FPO BA SozÖk** . 

(6)[1] The overall learning outcome of the specialization modules in socioeconomics is stipulated in Section 4 (1) and (6) **FPO BA SozÖk** .[ 3] Possible examinations for the other interdisciplinary specialization modules are: written examination (60 or 90 min), written assignment or seminar paper, or a combination of the same; Section 5 (2)(3) **BPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

(7)[1] The overall learning outcome of the stay abroad is to offer students the opportunity to explore interdisciplinary content and topics from business, economics and society that are not part of the home university’s main research focus and which therefore cannot be studied in depth at the home university.[2] Secondly, the stay abroad allows students to gain international experience and improve their intercultural skills.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice regarding which modules to take during the stay abroad offers students a wide range of specialization options.[4] Fourthly, the wide range of options allows students to shape their own particular profile in view of their future career (or consecutive studies).[5] The type and scope of the examination in the modules that may be taken during the stay abroad are stated in the module handbook, the examination regulations for the relevant subject or the host university abroad.[6] In order to achieve the learning outcome of the stay abroad, students must complete it in a country other than the one in which they obtained their university entrance qualification. 

(8)[1] Specialization modules usually consist of a lecture (4 SWS), a seminar (4 SWS) or a lecture and a tutorial (each 2 SWS).[2] Deviations are possible in the modules completed during the stay abroad.[3] Details are stipulated in the module handbook.

## **Section 4 Legal Validity** 

(1)[1] These degree program and examination regulations shall come into effect on October 1, 2024.[2] They shall apply to all students starting their degree program as of winter semester 2024/2025 and those students who at the time these regulations come into effect are already studying in accordance with a previously valid version of the degree program and examination regulations for the Bachelor's degree program in International Economic Studies at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at Friedrich Alexander Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – **FPO BSc IES** – dated September 2, 2020.[3] They shall apply to all students who have not yet started examinations for the module “Development economics” (first attempt).[4] Examinations according to the previously valid degree program examination regulations shall be offered for the last time in winter semester 2027/2028. 

(2) The degree program and examination regulations for the Bachelor's degree program in International Economic Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at Friedrich-AlexanderUniversität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – **FPO BSc IES** – dated September 2, 2020, last amended on March 23, 2023, shall become invalid as of March 31, 2028.
