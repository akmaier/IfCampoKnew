## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree programme and examination regulations for the research-oriented consecutive Master’s degree programme in International Business Studies leading to the qualification Master of Science supplements the general examination regulations for the Master’s degree programmes at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU – **MPOWISO** – in the currently valid version. 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master’s Degree Programme, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] The following shall be recognised as subject-related degrees within the meaning of Section 26 (1)(1) (1) alt.1 **MPOWISO** : Bachelor's degrees in the International Business Studies Bachelor’s degree programme at FAU within the meaning of **FPO BSc IBS** in the currently valid version.[2] Other degrees in the field of International Business Studies shall be considered equivalent within the meaning of Section 26 (2) **MPOWISO** , if students have acquired skills in the following fields with the following number of credits: a) at least 50 ECTS credits in Business Administration 

b) at least 10 ECTS credits in Statistics 

c) at least 10 ECTS credits in Culture and communication. 

3The following shall be recognised as subject-related degrees within the meaning of Section 26 (1)(1) (1) alt. 2 **MPOWISO** : Bachelor’s degrees in economics or social sciences as well as non-business degrees with subject-specific knowledge in economics worth at least 50 ECTS credits as well as knowledge in statistics worth at least 5 ECTS credits.[4] The final thesis is not included in the calculation of credits in accordance with sentences 2 and 3. 

(2) As further documentation within the meaning of 2.3.3 of the **Appendix** of **MPOWISO** , applicants shall be required to submit evidence of proficiency in English equivalent to at least level C1 of the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR); this proof shall be provided either with a certificate indicating that the applicant has successfully passed the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL), attaining at least 105 points in the iBT test or a score of 7.5 or above in the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) or by providing equivalent proof. 

(3) Applicants with an outstanding first degree pursuant to (1) sentences 1 and 2 shall be granted direct admission to the Master’s degree programme in IBS pursuant to 5.1 of the **Appendix** of **MPOWISO** if they obtained a grade of 1.3 or better in the relevant degree programme; Section 12 (4) sentences 1 to 3 **MPOWISO** shall apply accordingly for qualifications with a different grading system. 

(4) All applicants who are not granted direct admission to the Master’s degree programme pursuant to paragraph 3 must provide the following supporting documents as defined in 2.3.3 of the **Appendix** to **MPOWISO** : 

1. 1Evidence of time spent abroad (at least 3 months at a time) of relevance for the subject, in particular studying or completing an internship abroad or other work experience abroad; a transcript of records or another certificate from the foreign university or a reference or similar document from the employer where the applicant completed work experience or an internship can be accepted as proof. 2All work or study in international organisations or institutions that are not located in the applicant’s country of origin shall be considered relevant for the subject as defined in sentence 1.

2. 1Language skills certificates for languages other than English that indicate the level and scope of language training.[2] Proof of proficiency can be provided in the form of language certificates that state the CEFR level attained; the proof of proficiency in German must state the CEFR level attained. 

3. Presentation video (in a standard video format such as MPEG, AVI, etc. as well as by post on CD/DVD/USB, approximately 90 seconds), in which the applicant describes how their qualifications meet the requirements of the Master’s degree programme. 

(5)[1] Pursuant to 2.3 of the **Appendix** to **MPOWISO** and Section 4, the documents to be submitted by applicants whose overall grade in their first degree specified in paragraph 1 is between 1.31 and 2.50 shall be assessed on the second stage of the qualification assessment process pursuant to 5.2 of the **Appendix** to **MPOWISO** according to the following criteria: 

1. Quality of previous degree or previous achievements based on the overall grade average; assessment on the basis of graduation documents from first degree (max. 40 points), 

2. Scope of skills in business administration relevant for the degree programme; assessment on the basis of graduation documents from first degree (max. 10 points), 

3. Scope of skills in statistics relevant for the degree programme; assessment on the basis of graduation documents from first degree (max. 2.5 points), 

4. Quality of English language skills pursuant to paragraph 2 (max 20 points), 

5. Amount of experience abroad pursuant to paragraph 4 (1) (max. 10 points), 

6. Number of other languages spoken (except English) as well as quality of German language skills pursuant to paragraph 4 (2) (max. 7.5 points), 

7. Quality of the presentation video pursuant to paragraph 4 (3) using ‘substance’, ‘clarity’, ‘argumentation’ and ‘impression’ as criteria (2.5 points each, maximum 10 points in total).

## **Section 3 Scope and Structure of the Master’s Degree Programme, Examinations, Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1)[1] The core courses include in-depth study of the fundamentals of International Business Studies from the following nine areas; students must choose a total of 60 ECTS points from these modules: 

1. Environment of international business 

2. Foundations of international management 

3. International strategic management 

4. International functional management 

5. International information management 

6. Soft skills 

7. International finance and change management 

8. International corporate sustainability 

9. International relations. 

2In the electives, students must choose modules comprising 30 ECTS credits in one of the following area studies modules: 

1. English speaking countries 

2. Romance countries 

3. Asia 

4. Europe. 

3Students must also complete a Master's thesis, which comprises 30 ECTS credits. 

- 4The type and scope of the examinations are governed by the **Appendix** and Sections 16 – 18b **MPOWISO** . 

(2)[1] Section 4 (5) **MPOWISO** shall apply with the proviso that the teaching and examination language in the compulsory modules is English.[2] Elective modules may also be offered and chosen in which the teaching and examination language is German, Spanish or French. 

## **Section 4 Elective Modules** 

(1)[1] The aim of the module groups ‘English speaking countries’, ‘Romance countries’, ‘Asia’ and ‘Europe’ in the area studies electives pursuant to Section 3 (1) (2), is for students to gain sound knowledge of business, politics, culture and language in another region.[2] The aim of the regional skills gained is to prepare students for work in this particular region.[3] The content of all modules of the selected electives are related to the region in question.[4] Students can expand and build on the region-specific skills by completing study stays abroad. 

(2)[1] The type and scope of the examination are dependent on the skills for the chosen module according to (1) and the module handbook.[2] Possible examinations are: written examination (60 or 90 minutes), written assignment or seminar paper, presentation, case study as well as combinations of each of these forms.[3] The module catalogue is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice. 

(3)[1] The elective modules usually consist of either a lecture (1-3 SWS) or a tutorial (1-3 SWS).[2] Further details are stipulated in the module handbook.

## **Section 5 Legal Validity and Transitory Provisions** 

(1)[1] These degree programme and examination regulations shall come into effect on the day after their publication.[2] They shall apply to all students who enter the International Business Studies degree programme in the winter semester 2009/2010 or later. 

(2)[1] The ninth amendment statute shall come into effect on 1 October 2017.[2] It shall apply to all students starting a degree programme from winter semester 2017/2018 onwards. 

(3)[1] The tenth amendment statute shall come into effect on the day after its publication. 2It applies to all students who have not yet started examinations for the module ‘E-business strategy and networking’. 

(4)[1] The eleventh amendment statute shall come into effect on the day after its publication.[2] It shall apply to all students starting a degree programme from winter semester 2020/2021 onwards. 

(5)[1] The twelfth amendment statute shall come into effect on 1 October 2021.[2] It applies to all students who have not yet started examinations (first attempt) for the modules ‘Foundations of international management I’, ‘Foundations of international management II’, ‘International marketing’ and ‘Managing intercultural relations’. 3Examinations in accordance with the previously valid degree programme and examination regulations shall be offered for the last time in summer semester 2025 for the modules ‘Foundations of international management I’, ‘Foundations of international management II’, ‘International marketing’ and ‘Managing intercultural relations’.[4] From the date stated in sentence 3, those students who are affected by the examination regulations becoming invalid shall take their examinations in accordance with the currently valid version of the degree programme and examination regulations.
