## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree program and examination regulations for the research-oriented consecutive Master’s degree program in Finance, Auditing, Controlling, Taxation leading to the qualification Master of Science supplements the general examination regulations for the Master’s degree programs at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU – **MPOWISO** – in the currently valid version. 

## **Section 2 Start of Degree Program** 

The Master's degree program can also be started in the summer semester. 

## **Section 3 Admissions Committee for the Master’s Degree Program** 

1The admissions committee for the Master’s degree program FACT pursuant to Section 11 **MPOWISO** shall consist of three members.[2] The speaker of the FACT Institute shall chair the admissions committee.[3] The chairperson and one further member must be university lecturers as their principal employment.[4] The third member shall be a research associate from the FACT Institute.[5] In the event that there are over 100 applicants, the Faculty Council of the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law may increase the number of members of the admissions committee pursuant to sentence 1 by one member for every 25 applications; when appointing members, it must be ensured that at least half of the votes remain with the group of members who are lecturers as their main occupation.[6] The admissions committee shall appoint a selection committee consisting of four professors from the FACT Institute, one from each of the four FACT areas, who are all eligible to act as examiners in the degree program in Finance,

Auditing, Controlling, Taxation.[7] The members of the selection committee shall be responsible for assessing the discussion of the FACT-related papers from their own area of expertise; Section 3 (4) shall apply.[8] The admissions committee shall take the final decision about admission, but if there is a tie in votes, the chairperson’s vote shall be decisive.

## **Section 5 Scope and Structure of the Master’s Degree Program, Examinations, Related Degree Programs** 

(1)[1] The compulsory modules involve in-depth study of fundamental interdisciplinary skills (30 ECTS credits).[2] In addition, students select modules from a range of specialization modules (each worth 5 ECTS credits) that make up a total of 60 ECTS credits from various module groups.[3] The module handbook contains suggestions for students of potential professions for each module (e.g. tax advisor, investment banker, auditor) to assist them with their selections.[4] The Master’s thesis module consists of completing the Master’s thesis (30 ECTS credits).[5] The distribution of modules across the semesters, the type and scope of the examinations in the modules and the required number of ECTS credits are set forth in **Appendix 1** and **2** as well as Sections 4a and 16 to 18b of **MPOWISO** . 

(2) The provisions in Section 27 (1)(3)(2) **MPOWISO** shall not apply to related degree programs. 

## **Section 6 Specialization Modules** 

(1)[1] The overriding learning outcome of the module groups “Finance and insurance”, “Auditing and law”, “Controlling”, “Taxation” and “Interdisciplinary modules” of the specialization modules is firstly to enable students to chose an individual focus from the freely available modules from one or more of the module groups and thus allow them to explore one or more areas in more depth.[2] A further learning outcome is methodological, providing interdisciplinary training as well as extending comparative approaches to other fields of study.[3] Thirdly, the element of choice in the specialization modules gives students the opportunity to create their own particular skills profile in view of their future career. 

4 

(2)[1] The learning outcome of the module group “Finance and insurance” is to enable students to identify tasks and issues relating to finance and insurance and provide them with methods suitable for solving these. 

(3) The learning outcome of the module group “Auditing and law” is to enable students to identify tasks and issues relating to company law, accounting and auditing and provide them with methods suitable for solving these. 

(4) The learning outcome of the module group “Controlling” is to enable students to apply suitable theories, concepts and instruments to provide external and internal stakeholders with relevant information for controlling companies, allowing the necessary decisions to be taken in an informed manner. 

(5) The learning outcome of the module group “Taxation” is to enable students to identify tasks and issues relating to tax law and business taxation and provide them with methods suitable for solving these. 

(6) The learning outcome of the module group “Interdisciplinary modules” is to enable students to identify issues of relevance to FACT such as sustainability, digitalization and statistical methods and provide them with methods suitable for solving these. 

(7)[1] The type and scope of the examination depend on the skills for the chosen module according to (2) to (6) and are stipulated in the module handbook.[2] The following types of examination are permitted for specialization modules offered by the chairs in the FACT Institute in the module groups “Finance and insurance”, “Auditing and law”, “Controlling” and “Taxation”: Written examination, seminar paper, presentation, handout or a combination of these; Section 6 (2)(3) **MPOWISO** shall remain unaffected.[3] This shall also apply to the modules “Current topics from FACT I”,”Current topics from FACT II”, “Current topics from FACT III“, “Key qualifications FACT I” and “Key qualifications FACT II” from the module group “Interdisciplinary modules”.[4] For modules imported from other institutes, subjects, and faculties, the type and scope of the examination shall depend either on the relevant module description or on the degree program and examination regulations applicable to the degree program from which the module is taken.[5] The module catalog is announced before the beginning of the semester in accordance with local practice. 

(8)[1] Compulsory elective modules usually consist of a lecture (1-2 SWS) and a tutorial/exercise (1-2 SWS) or a seminar (2-4 SWS).[2] Details are stipulated in the module handbook. 

## **Section 7 Legal Validity and Transitory Provisions** 

(1)[1] These degree program and examination regulations shall come into effect on October 1, 2023.[2] They shall apply to all students who start the Finance, Auditing, Controlling, Taxation Master’s degree program in winter semester 2023/2024 or later. 

(2)[1] Once these regulations come into effect, the degree program and examination regulations for the Master’s degree program in Finance, Auditing, Controlling, Taxation at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – FPOFACT – dated July 20, 2009, last amended by the statute dated July 21, 2022, shall become invalid.[2] Students already studying under one of the previously valid versions of the degree program and examination regulations
