## **Section 1 Scope** 

The degree program and examination regulations for the research-oriented consecutive Master’s degree program in International Business Studies leading to the qualification Master of Science supplements the general examinations regulations for the Master’s degree programs at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at FAU – **MPOWISO** – in the currently valid version. 

## **Section 2 Qualification for a Master’s Degree Program, Certificates and Admission Requirements** 

(1)[1] The following shall be recognized as subject-related degrees within the meaning of Section 32 (1)(1) (1) alt.1 **MPOWISO** : Bachelor's degrees in the International Business Studies Bachelor's degree program at FAU within the meaning of **FPO BSc IBS** in the currently valid version.[2] Other degrees in the field of International Business Studies shall be considered subject-related and equivalent within the meaning of Section 32 (2) **MPOWISO** , if students have acquired skills in the following fields with the following number of credits: 

a) at least 50 ECTS credits in Business Administration 

b) at least 10 ECTS credits in Statistics 

c) at least 10 ECTS credits in culture and communication.

## **Section 3 Scope and Structure of the Master’s Degree Program, Examinations, Teaching and Examination Language** 

(1)[1] The core courses include in-depth interdisciplinary study of the fundamentals of International Business Studies from the following nine areas; students must choose a total of 60 ECTS points from these modules: 

1. Environment of international business 

2. Foundations of international management 

3. International strategic management 

4. International functional management 

5. International information management 

6. Soft skills 

7. International finance and change management 

8. International corporate sustainability 

9. International relations. 

2Further information about the individual areas within the meaning of sentence 1 is specified in Section 4. 

(2)[ 1] In the electives, students must choose modules comprising 30 ECTS credits in one of the following area studies modules: 

1. English speaking countries 

2. Latin America 

3. Asia 

4. Europe. 

2Students must also complete a Master's thesis, which comprises 30 ECTS credits. 

> 3The type and scope of the examinations are governed by the **Appendix** and Sections 17 – 24 **MPOWISO** . 

(3)[1] Section 4 (5) **MPOWISO** shall apply with the proviso that the teaching and examination language in the compulsory modules is English.[2] Elective modules may also be offered and chosen in which the teaching and examination language is German, Spanish or French. 

## **Section 4 Core Courses Within the Meaning of Section 3 (1)** 

(1 )[1] The main learning outcome of the core course “Environment of international business” is to enable students to analyze the political, economic and legal frameworks for multi-national companies in various countries.[2] They should be capable of evaluating the effects of global developments on companies and international markets and of developing suitable strategies for dealing with these challenges.[ 3] The specific learning outcomes and examination subjects for the individual modules depend on the skills taught in the chosen module and are stipulated in the module handbook.[4] The examination type is a written examination (60 min).

(2)[1] The main learning outcome of the core course "Foundations of international management" is to enable students to use fundamental theories, concepts and instruments from international management.[2] They should be able to identify complex challenges in management in a multi-cultural and global environment and to solve these challenges through the use of suitable management methods.[ 3] The specific learning outcomes and examination subjects as well as type and scope of the examination for the individual modules depend on the skills taught in the chosen module and are stipulated in the module handbook.[4] Examinations shall take one of the following forms: Handout or presentation, or a combination of these; Section 6 (2)(3) **MPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

(3)[1] The main learning outcome of the core course "International strategic management" is to enable students to analyze strategic challenges on an international level and to make informed strategic decisions.[2] They should be able to evaluate various market conditions and competitive situations and to develop appropriate strategies for the success of international companies.[3] The specific learning outcomes and examination subjects as well as type and scope of the examination for the individual modules depend on the skills taught in the chosen module and are stipulated in the module handbook.[4] Examinations shall take one of the following forms: Written examination (60 min) or handout, or a combination of these; Section 6 (2)(3) **MPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

(4)[1] The main learning outcome of the core course "International functional management" is to enable students to understand the functional areas of a company, such as marketing, operations and finance, in an international context and to manage them effectively.[2] They should be able to identify problems relevant to all functions and to develop function-specific and integrative solutions using suitable methods.[ 3] The specific learning outcomes and examination subjects as well as type and scope of the examination for the individual modules depend on the skills taught in the chosen module and are stipulated in the module handbook.[4] Examinations shall take one of the following forms: Handout or presentation, or a combination of these; Section 6 (2)(3) **MPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

(5)[1] The main learning outcome of the core course "International information management" is to enable students to understand the role of information technology and innovation management in the international business environment.[2] They should be able to develop IT-based strategies for supporting business processes and to shape digital transformation in international organizations.[3] The specific learning outcomes and examination subjects as well as type and scope of the examination for the individual modules depend on the skills taught in the chosen module and are stipulated in the module handbook.[4] Examinations shall take one of the following forms: Presentation or seminar paper, or a combination of these; Section 6 (2)(3) **MPOWISO** shall remain unaffected. 

(6)[1] The main learning outcome of the core course "Soft skills” is to enable students to develop their intercultural communication and teamworking skills.[2] They should be able to work effectively in multi-cultural teams and to use their leadership skills in an international environment.[3] The specific learning outcomes and examination subjects as

**Section 5 Elective Modules: Area Studies Within the Meaning of Section 3 (2)** (1)[1] The main learning outcome of the individual area studies “English speaking countries”, “Latin America”, “Asia” and “Europe” pursuant to Section 3 (2), is for students to gain sound knowledge of business, politics, culture and language in another region.[2] The aim of the regional skills gained is to prepare students for work in this particular region and to develop a skills profile in business and economics suited to their future profession.[3] The content of all modules of the selected electives are related to the relevant regions pursuant to Section 3 (2).[4] Students can expand and build on the region-specific skills by completing study stays abroad. 

(2)[1] Area studies "English-speaking countries" focuses on the economic and political dynamics of English-speaking countries such as the USA, the United Kingdom, Canada and Australia.[2] Topics such as international organizations, trade and innovation

(3)[1] Area studies "Latin America" focuses on the economic, political, social and environmental challenges faced by countries in Latin America such as Argentina, Brazil, Bolivia or Peru.[2] Topics such as global supply chains, sustainability governance, environmental justice and human rights are investigated.[3] Particular emphasis is placed on the relationship of Latin America with global stakeholders such as the USA, the EU and China as well as on the analysis of solutions for social and environmental problems in the region. 

(4)[1] Area studies "Asia" focuses on the economic, technical and cultural development of countries in Asia, and on China and India in particular.[2] Topics such as digital transformation and challenges for international management are investigated.[3] The aim is to understand the role of countries like China and India in the global economy and to develop innovative approaches to solve management problems in Asian contexts. 

(5)[1] Area studies "Europe" focuses on the economic, political and social challenges faced by the EU as well as those of individual countries in Europe such as Germany, France, or Spain.[2] Topics such as sustainability management, climate policy and digital transformation in Germany and other European countries are investigated.[3] The aim is to analyze strategies for decarbonization and the development of sustainable industries, with a particular focus on the role of digital innovations and technologies for overcoming the challenges faced by Europe and the world as a whole.

(8)[1] The elective modules usually consist of either a lecture (1-3 SWS) or a tutorial (13 SWS).[2] Further details are stipulated in the module handbook. 

## **Section 6 Legal Validity and Transitory Provisions** 

(1 )[1] These degree program and examination regulations shall come into effect on April 1, 2025.[2] They shall apply to all students who enter the Master’s degree program in International Business Studies as of winter semester 2025/2026. 

(2)[1] The degree program and examination regulations for the Master’s degree program in International Business Studies at the School of Business, Economics and Society at the Faculty of Business, Economics, and Law at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) – **FPO MSc IBS** – dated July 24, 2007, last amended on August 8, 2022, shall become invalid as of September 30, 2028.[2] Examinations according to the versions of the degree program and examination regulations valid before July 26, 2021 stated in sentence 1 shall be offered for the last time in summer semester 2028.
